Energy market predictability

Forecasting the German day-ahead power market, and measuring where forecasts fail. Real market data, explicit baselines, statistics that are allowed to say "no effect".

Three questions drove this project: can you predict tomorrow's cheapest electricity hours, what is that prediction worth in euros, and can a scheduled mega-event (the 2026 World Cup) move the market? The answers, computed live from the latest pipeline run:

Product implication: a smart-charging feature on this market needs automation and UX, not machine learning. The algorithm choice moves a few euros a year; automated timing at all moves about two hundred.

The pipeline: aWATTar and ENTSO-E data, a walk-forward backtest against naive baselines, matched comparable-day event studies with placebo tests, published as a static page a GitHub Actions job refreshes daily.
